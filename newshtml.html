20/09/2022 Gymnastics history makers the toast of Sofia Rhythmic Worlds

That the Mediterranean wind that whirled through Sofia’s (BUL) Arena Armeec last week was generated by a young woman also called Sofia is one of the quirks of a World Championships that will go down as one of the most exciting ever. With six of a possible nine golds, Italian gymnasts led by superstar Sofia Raffaeli (ITA) were the dominant force of a World Championships unlike any that has come before. Raffaeli, the most successful gymnast of the World Cup series in 2022, lived up to her pre-competition hype, winning titles with the Hoop, Ball, and Ribbon, then capturing the All-Around.

17/09/2022 Stellar in Sofia, Raffaeli romps to World All-Around title in Rhythmic Gymnastics

Sofia Raffaeli (ITA) became Italy’s first World All-Around champion in Individual Rhythmic Gymnastics Saturday evening in Sofia (BUL), capping off a remarkable World Championship run in which the 18-year-old superstar took five of a possible six gold medals. After golds with the Team, Hoop, Ball, and Ribbon and a bronze with the Clubs earlier in the week, Raffaeli was the odds-on favorite for All-Around gold heading into Saturday’s final. Victory crowned her in the end, though not before the 18-year-old found herself in the unfamiliar position of trailing fellow contender Darja Varfolomeev (GER) before each gymnast’s final routine. The new World champion with the Clubs, 15-year-old Varfolomeev has been sublime throughout the week, seeming to draw strength with every performance. On Saturday, Varfolomeev started with her three strongest apparatus, building a one-point lead with flawless, difficulty-packed routines with the Hoop, Ball, and Clubs.
